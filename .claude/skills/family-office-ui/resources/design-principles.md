# Design principles

## Brand premise
Mathieson Family should feel like a private digital estate: part family archive, part operating system, part advisory room.
The aesthetic target is closer to a discreet family office, private members club, or well-designed editorial magazine than a startup dashboard.

## Tone words
- Restrained
- Private
- Multi-generational
- Editorial
- Cultured
- Organized
- Calm
- High-trust

## Mode model

### 1. Family mode
Use for profiles, family tree, galleries, stories, milestones, travel logs, and memory surfaces.

Visual cues:
- warmer neutrals
- larger image moments
- softer edges
- more generous whitespace
- layered content rather than dense utilities
- captions and metadata that feel like an archive

Think:
- coffee-table book
- boutique hotel members portal
- digital family album with taste

### 2. Operations mode
Use for homes, vehicles, vendors, maintenance, reservations, logistics, and household systems.

Visual cues:
- more obvious structure
- grids, maps, schedules, and tables used carefully
- fewer decorative moves
- stronger subdivision and wayfinding
- spatial context, ownership context, and status context visible at a glance

Think:
- luxury property management dashboard
- architecture office project board
- premium travel concierge operations panel

### 3. Advisory mode
Use for trust planning, documents, distributions, governance, advisors, summaries, and finance-related views.

Visual cues:
- document and dossier feel
- stronger typographic hierarchy
- tighter spacing in content areas, but never cramped
- subtle rules, fine borders, quiet emphasis
- information presented as briefs, memos, or packets

Think:
- private bank portal
- board memo
- family governance handbook

## Palette guidance
Default to warm neutrals with deep ink text.
Recommended accent families:
- Forest / olive for property and stewardship
- Deep teal for platform-wide navigation and selected actions
- Burgundy / oxblood for archival or formal moments
- Bronze / stone for subtle premium detailing

Rules:
- one accent family per screen
- avoid saturated blues and violets
- avoid high-contrast gradient backgrounds
- use color to separate modes, not to decorate randomly

## Typography guidance
Suggested pairing:
- Display: Canela, Ivar Display, Cormorant Garamond, or Instrument Serif
- UI/body: Inter, Söhne, Manrope, or Source Sans/General Sans equivalent

Usage:
- Display serif only for hero titles, section openers, and occasional panel headings
- Sans for all navigation, controls, tables, and body copy
- Never use the serif for small UI labels

## Layout guidance
- Left-align almost everything
- Use asymmetric composition where possible
- Prefer one dominant content block plus 1–2 supporting blocks
- Break pages into chapters rather than endless utility cards
- Use full-bleed or near-full-bleed imagery only in family mode and selected landing moments

## Motion guidance
- slow, subtle, elegant
- fades, slides, and reveals should feel physical but quiet
- hover states should be understated
- no bouncing, springy toy-like interactions for core product surfaces

## What luxury looks like here
Luxury is not excess.
Luxury here means:
- calm spacing
- excellent typography
- fewer but better containers
- real hierarchy
- clear separation between emotional and administrative content
- confidence not clutter
